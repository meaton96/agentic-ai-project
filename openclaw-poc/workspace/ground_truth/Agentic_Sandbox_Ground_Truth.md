# Agentic Sandbox Project (AIOS) - Ground Truth Reference Document

## 1. Project Vision and Motivation
* **Core Concept**: Design and build an industry-informed agentic innovation infrastructure, referred to as an Agentic Operating System (AIOS) or "Sandbox".
* **Vision**: Create a controlled, isolated environment where deployed AI agents can execute tasks (perceive, reason, act) and collaborate with other agents without producing unintended consequences or affecting real-world enterprise systems.
* **Motivation**: As organizations shift towards enterprise systems composed of multiple autonomous agents, the Sandbox answers a critical architectural question: *How do we architect these systems well?* It allows organizations to safely address "what-if" scenarios. Furthermore, it facilitates the translation of university-based AI inventions into practical industry solutions.
* **Target Partners**: University researchers, industry organizations, non-profit organizations, and government entities.

## 2. Core Features and Capabilities
The AIOS Sandbox will be designed to offer the following high-level capabilities:
* **Multi-Agent Simulation**: Simulate complex AI solutions composed of a network of collaborating AI agents.
* **Workflow Orchestration**: Orchestrate complex, multistep workflows with intricate dependencies, all driven by a defined goal or outcome.
* **Architecture Optimization**: Provide system-level goal-seeking capabilities to optimize agentic architectures and address architectural design flaws.
* **Continuous Improvement**: Enable self-improving mechanisms via reinforcement learning (RL) and human observation.
* **Risk Evaluation**: Simulate and evaluate cyber risk metrics specific to multi-agent environments.
* **Explainability & Agency**: Implement built-in capabilities to address and ensure AI explainability and maintain proper agent agency.
* **Human Team Mirroring**: Foster widespread adoption of sophisticated multi-agent systems equipped with specialized skills that mirror human team dynamics.

## 3. System Architecture & Context
The infrastructure of the Agentic Sandbox is conceptualized through the following layers:
* **Computing Infrastructure**: Built upon an advanced or enhanced selection of open-source frameworks.
* **Agentic OS / Infrastructure**: The core engine managing the deployment, integration, and execution of autonomous agents.
* **Input Streams**:
    * *Business/Societal Challenges*: Use cases driven by industry, non-profits, and government entities.
    * *Agentic Innovations*: Single or multi-agent research models generated by RIT AI Research and university partners.
* **Industry Verticals**: Integration of specialized industry capabilities and skills.

**RIT Strategic Pipeline (Translation)**
A major theme of the project is bridging the gap between theoretical academia and industry. The project supports RIT's strategic direction of driving practice-inspired AI research. It aims to translate new algorithms and faculty-led research (inventions) into tangible commercial and societal artifacts through the university's entrepreneurial ecosystem and technology transfer offices.

## 4. Research Directions, Milestones, & State of the Art
### Immediate Summer Goals
* **Vision Paper**: The primary end-of-summer objective is to author a vision paper describing the Agentic Sandbox. This will define the agent OS, detail key functions/requirements, map out the technical systems architecture, highlight 2-3 representative use cases, and outline next steps.
* **Prototyping & Infrastructure Evaluation**: 
    * Survey and evaluate leading infrastructure and tools for agent building and deployment (e.g., OpenClaw, Manus, OpenAI Operator). Compare and contrast these tools.
    * Build a prototype agent to demonstrate infrastructure capabilities (led by a summer student).
* **Use Case Identification**: Establish 2-3 specific scenarios to test the infrastructure. Current targeted domains include:
    * Healthcare
    * Cyber risks in multi-agentic systems

### Fall Goals
* Deepen engagement with partner universities and industry stakeholders.
* Develop a formal funding proposal for agencies such as NIST and/or NSF.

## 5. Evaluation Criteria for Research Papers (Agent Ground Truth)
*When using an LLM agent to review and compare research literature against this project, papers should be evaluated on their relevance to the following core investigation areas:*

1. **Agent-to-Agent (A2A) Protocols**: Literature discussing emerging communication protocols and standards between autonomous agents.
2. **Workflow Orchestration**: Frameworks and methods for the construction and orchestration of multi-agent workflows.
3. **System-Level Intent & Recommendation**: System-level approaches for building architectures (e.g., *From Intent to Execution: Composing Agentic Workflows with Agent Recommendations* / AutoMAS).
4. **Adaptive Systems (RL & Control Theory)**: The application of Reinforcement Learning (RL) and control-theoretic approaches for creating adaptive, self-improving agentic systems.
5. **Systems Theory**: The utilization of general systems theory in the design and building of AI agent-based systems.
